Boyfriend to the Couple

  • 2 oz. bourbon
  • ½ oz. Averna
  • 4 dashes Angostura bitters
  • 1 dash orange bitters
  • stir, serve on one rock
  • lemon zest for garnish

A while back I saw someone else invent this drink during a winter ice storm and had named it the "Busted Pipe". Whatever you call it—a personal favorite.

Godfather

  • 2 oz. scotch
  • ¼ oz. amaretto
  • stir, serve up or on one rock
  • lemon zest for garnish

You don't need a fancy scotch to have a good time—blended will do just fine in this cocktail.

King Cohen

  • 2 oz. scotch whisky
  • 1 barspoon fernet
  • ½ barspoon simple syrup (1:1)
  • stir, serve on one rock
  • lime wedge for garnish

Fernet is extremely minty, but once you get a taste for it, it'll never let go. The lime garnish is unusual for a bourbon-forward beverage, but trust!

Old '97

  • 1½ oz. bourbon
  • ¾ oz. simple syrup (1:1)
  • ¾ oz. maraschino liqueur
  • 2 dashes Angostura bitters
  • stir, serve on one rock
  • cocktail cherry for garnish

What a can of Coca-Cola wishes it was.

Old Fashioned

  • 2 oz. bourbon
  • 1 barspoon demerara sugar (or one sugar cube, or ½ oz. simple syrup)
  • 4 bitters
  • stir, serve on one rock
  • orange zest and cocktail cherry for garnish

You can make an Old Fashioned with any spirit, bitters or sugar you like. Choose a sugar and garnish that complements the spirit. For example, rye works better with lemon zest. Mezcal prefers agave syrup and grapefruit zest.

Re-animator

  • 1½ oz. rye
  • 1½ oz. Amaro Nonino
  • stir, serve on one rock
  • lemon zest for garnish

Amaro Nonino is wonderful all on its own, but the bite of a spicy rye lets you enjoy its smooth aftertaste even more.

Rusty Nail

  • 2 oz. scotch
  • 1 oz. Drambuie
  • stir, serve on one rock
  • lemon zest for garnish

I prefer a 2:1 ratio of scotch to Drambuie, but some people like their nail sweeter, with an equal ratio.

Sentimental Gentleman

  • 2 oz. scotch
  • ½ oz. Bénédictine
  • ½ oz. walnut liqueur
  • stir, serve on one rock
  • lemon zest for garnish

Walnut liqueurs make excellent digestif on their own, and this drink inherits that properly—the perfect dessert beverage.

Aviation

  • 2 oz. dry gin
  • ¼ oz. crème de violette
  • ¼ oz. maraschino liqueur
  • ½ oz. lemon juice
  • stir, serve up
  • cocktail cherry and lemon zest for garnish

It's unusual to stir a cocktail with citrus juice in it, but this one works—and it's a relatively small amount.

You can shake this cocktail if you prefer, but the result will be greyer in color and lighter in texture, like an overcast day.

Deep Blue Sea

  • 2 oz. dry gin
  • ¾ oz. dry Lillet Blanc
  • ¼ oz. crème de violette
  • 1 dash orange bitters
  • stir, serve up
  • lemon zest for garnish

Martini

  • 2½ oz. dry gin
  • ½ oz. dry vermouth
  • 1 dash orange bitters
  • stir, serve up
  • 1 or 3 olives for garnish

The martini has many variants. The "dryness" of a martini is measured by the ratio of gin to vermouth. This base recipe is quite dry, at a 5:1 ratio. In addition to the Perfect Martini, there are a few important variants:

  • 50/50 Martini: Use 1½ oz. gin and 1½ oz. vermouth instead.
  • Dirty Martini: Omit bitters and add 1 barspoon–½ oz. olive brine.
  • Gibson Martini: Garnish with 1 or 3 cocktail onions instead.
  • Vodka Martini: Any martini can be made with vodka instead of gin—dirty martinis, especially, since the more neutral flavor profile doesn't clash with salty brine the way that some gins might.

Martinez

  • 1¼ oz. old tom gin
  • 1¼ oz. sweet vermouth
  • ¼ oz. maraschino liqueur
  • ¼ oz. orange bitters
  • stir, serve up
  • lemon zest for garnish

Perfect Martini

  • 1½ oz. dry gin
  • ¾ oz. dry vermouth
  • ¾ oz. sweet vermouth
  • 1 dash orange bitters
  • stir, serve up
  • lemon zest for garnish

You can imagine how many inexperienced bartenders have been humiliated by a customer ordering this variant and complaining "it's not perfect"!

Rum

Cuba Libre

  • 2 oz. white rum
  • ½ oz. lime juice
  • 4 oz. cola
  • mix in glass and stir
  • lime wheel for garnish

Use a cane sugar cola if you can.

Daiquiri

  • 2 oz. white rum
  • 1 oz. lime juice
  • ½ oz. demerara syrup (2:1)
  • shake, double strain, serve up

Simple perfection.

Bijou

  • 1¼ oz. dry gin
  • ¾ oz. sweet vermouth
  • ¼ oz. green chartreuse
  • 1 dash orange bitters
  • stir, serve up
  • lemon zest and cocktail cherry for garnish

Corpse Reviver no. 2

  • ¾ oz. dry gin
  • ¾ oz. Lillet Blanc
  • ¾ oz. orange liqueur
  • ¾ oz. lemon juice
  • absinthe for glass rinse
  • wet shake, double strain, serve up

Satan's Whiskers

  • ¾ oz. gin
  • ¾ oz. dry vermouth
  • ¾ oz. sweet vermouth
  • ½ oz. Grand Marnier
  • ½ oz. orange juice
  • 1 dash orange bitters
  • wet shake, double strain, serve up
  • orange zest for garnish

This recipe is for "straight whiskers".

  • Curled Whiskers: Subtitute Dry Curaçao for Grand Marnier.

Sazerac

  • 1½ oz. rye
  • ½ oz. cognac
  • ¼ oz. simple syrup (1½:1)
  • 3 dashes Peychaud's bitters
  • 1 dash Angostura bitters
  • absinthe for glass rinse
  • stir, serve
  • lemon zest for garnish

You can omit the rye and use 2 oz. of cognac instead.

Scofflaw

  • 1½ oz. rye
  • 1 oz. dry vermouth
  • ½ oz. lemon juice
  • ¼ oz. grenadine
  • 2 dashes orange bitters
  • stir, serve
  • orange zest for garnish

You can omit the rye and use 2 oz. of cognac instead.

Sidecar

  • 1½ oz. cognac
  • 1 oz. orange liqueur
  • ½ oz. lemon juice
  • white sugar for rim
  • shake, double strain
  • lemon zest for garnish

Vieux Carré

  • ¾ oz. rye whiskey
  • ¾ oz. cognac
  • ¾ oz. sweet vermouth
  • ½ oz. Bénédictine
  • 2 dashes Peychaud's bitters
  • 2 dashes Angostura bitters
  • stir, serve
  • lemon zest for garnish

You can serve this on ice, but it's best enjoyed in a small glass with no ice—for example, a 4 oz. shot glass.

Elderflower Martini

  • 3 oz. gin
  • 6 thinly sliced cucumbers wheels, muddled
  • ¾ oz. elderflower liqueur
  • ¼ oz. lemon juice,
  • shake, serve up

You can substitute vodka for gin, but…why?

Kentucky Flyer

  • 2 oz. rye
  • ¾ oz. maraschino liqueur
  • ½ oz. lemon juice
  • shake, double strain, serve up
  • mint sprig and 2 cocktail cherries for garnish

Fernet is extremely minty, but once you get a taste for it, it'll never let go. The lime garnish is unusual for a bourbon-forward beverage, but trust!

Kentucky Maid

  • 2 oz. bourbon
  • ½ oz. elderflower liqueur
  • ¾ oz. lemon juice
  • ¾ oz. simple syrup (1:1)
  • shake, double strain, serve over one rock
  • 2-3 thin cucumber slices for garnish

Irish Maid: Use Irish whisky instead of the bourbon.

Midnight Stinger

  • 1 oz. bourbon
  • 1 oz. fernet
  • ¾ oz. lemon juice
  • ¾ oz. simple syrup (1:1)
  • shake, double strain, serve over crushed ice
  • mint sprig for garnish

This is probably the best introductions to fernet for a burbon lover—like sweet tea in summer.

Pegu Club

  • 2 oz. dry gin
  • ¾ oz. orange curaçao
  • ½ oz. lime juice
  • 1 dash Angostura bitters
  • 1 dash orange bitters
  • shake, double strain, serve up

Whiskey Smash

  • 2 oz. bourbon
  • ½ oz. simple syrup (2:1)
  • 4 lemon slices (de-seeded and muddled)
  • 4-8 mint leaves
  • shake, double strain, serve on one rock
  • lemon wheel and mint sprig for garnish

Like the Old Fashioned, the smash is extremely versatile. Make it any fruit, citrus, spirit, or herb combination that pleases you!
